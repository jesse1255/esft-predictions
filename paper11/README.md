# Paper XI — Scattering Amplitudes from Topological Soliton Interactions

**Journal:** Physical Review D (in preparation)

## Summary

Develops a scattering amplitude formalism connecting topological soliton interactions
to measurable cross sections. Makes five testable predictions.

### Five Testable Predictions

1. **π-π scattering confirmation** — precision measurement at 2.8% level
2. **q₆ resonance at 65.78 GeV** — J^P = 1/2⁻, Γ ≈ 3.2 MeV, no SM counterpart (LHC search target)
3. **BKT phase transition at √s = 918 MeV** — discontinuity in π-π scattering
4. **Electron form factor** — 0.38% deviation at 10 GeV, 9.6% at 50 GeV (Belle II, BES III)
5. **Reflectionless scattering** — in analog condensed matter systems

### Exact Soliton Form Factor

F(q) = (πqa/2) / sinh(πqa/2)

Exponential UV suppression (not polynomial) — fully compatible with LEP data at 200 GeV.
